What is ICO and pre-ICO?

ICO, or Initial Coin Offering is a mechanism for raising funds, under the terms of which future crypto currency is sold for liquid virtual money. You invest bitcoin or ether in ICO, and in return receive tokens of the new project. This is another realization of the crowdfunding model. The participants finance the company's development now in order to obtain from it some benefit in the future.

Pre-ICO - stage before ICO (main stage), fundraising for the project. Pre-ICO is conducted to test and verify the demand for the project, get support from the community and expand the budget for marketing before the main ICO. Not all projects carry out pre-ICO.

What is crowdfunding campaign?

This is a mechanism for attracting finance from the masses with the aim of selling the product, holding events, supporting businesses, etc. There are 3 models of Crowdfunding according to the type of sponsor rewards: No reward (donations), Non-financial reward (Kickstarter model), Financial reward (Crowdinvesting). Crowdinvesting is divided into the Royalty model, People's lending and Joint crowdfunding.

What is crowdinvesting campaign?

This is an alternative financial tool for attracting capital to start-ups and small businesses from a wide range of micro-investors. Investors receive a share in the share capital of the company and the risk of loss of investment. However, the size of the possible profit isn’t fixed.

What is blockchain?

Blockchain is a distributed database that doesn’t have storage devices connected to a shared server. This database stores an ever-growing list of ordered records, called blocks. Each block contains a timestamp and a link to the previous block.

What is smart-contract?

This is a program code that allows you to automatically perform certain functions when certain conditions occur. To implement smart-contracts, a decentralized environment is required that completely excludes the human factor. To use the transfer of value in a smart-contract, it requires a cryptocurrency.

What is cryptocurrency?

These are monetary units (also known as "coins" or "tokens") protected by cryptographic technology. There is no physical analogue to these monetary units, they exist only in virtual space. Tokens are protected from forgery, because they represent encrypted information that can not be copied (the use of cryptography defined the prefix "crypto" in the title).

Crowdfunding

This is the usual crowdfunding campaign, the authors of which create a description and promise rewards for support. The authors pay the platform a 4% commission from fundraising. pre-ICO and ICO

ICO = Initial Coin Offering. These campaigns necessarily have their own cryptocurrency (token), which are received by those who supported them financially, in addition to the KickCoins, which reward the participants of the campaigns platform. Commission platform for the launch of ICO or pre-ICO on the platform - 4% of fees and 4% in the campaign tokens. 8% of KickCoins are generated for the commission received and only for the successful completion of the campaign (thus, KickCoins are secured not only by the success of the campaign and the author's' work, but also by real money and tokens of this campaign - in fact, by 4% + 4%, KICKICO sells its currency, KickCoins). These 8% are distributed among the participants in the ICO campaign in accordance with their payments.

Crowdinvesting

Unlike ICO, the tokens of the crowdinvesting campaign are limited to a predetermined number and can not exceed 100%. 100% of tokens = represent 100% of the company. The author himself decides what percentage of the company he wants to sell (for example, 40%) and for how much. Later, 40% will be distributed among those who bought these tokens,when making a profit, in accordance with their shares. Members of crowdinvesting campaigns also receive KickCoins.

  2. In the next step, you will need to keep your secret key encrypted. This is the same as the key to the safe, but in electronic form.     2. Select Private Key. Here you will have to enter your unencrypted private address and password will not be required. This is a dangerous and unprotected way, the use of which we do not recommend! This key must be stored in a secure offline store and used only as a last resort.

How to take KickCoin in your project (store, game, somewhere else)?

There are two ways. One simple, the second more complicated. Depending on what exactly you need in the project, you can choose one of them.

 

  1. You create a smart-contract that accepts KickCoins. Each time, when taking KickCoin from a specific wallet address, the smart-contract sends this address to your project (game, store, etc.). Next, it checks who owns this address and receives the goods purchased by him for KickCoin (game currency, product copy, T-shirt, etc.). Simultaneously, a smart-contract sends KickCoin to your company's wallet.
  2. This method is more complex and suits you if you want to not just take KickCoin and sell products for them, but want to make a KickCoin turn in your project. For example, if this is a game, then your players can create a real economy in it, buy and sell goods for KickCoin and thus earn real money in the game. In order to realize this, you can create unique KickCoin wallets for your players, which players can replenish and then use their usual accounts within your project. Alternatively, if you need total control over the accounts of your users, then you accept KickCoin for a smart-contract, and users within the project receive a copy of the cryptocurrency. In this case, you can define all actions performed by your users. The downside of this implementation is that users will have to transfer KickCoins to their personal wallet outside of your product,to use their KickCoin somewhere else.

What is the impact of the decision by the SEC and Singapore to qualify the ICO as a valuable papers with appropriate regulation?

The question isn’t quite right, since the SEC decision about "The DAO" from 25/07/17 and the clarification of MAS on the issue of ICO regulation from 01/08/17 don’t qualify all ICOs as a valuable papers.

Both documents explain that a token can be qualified as a valuable paper if the issue and sale of crypto tokens occurs in exchange for promises of a share in the company and/or the receipt of dividends and/or other such obligation.

According to the explanations of MAS from 01/08/17 "The types of digital tokens offered in Singapore and other countries vary widely. Some offers of tokens may fall under the regulation of the Securities and Futures Act (SFA), while others may not fall. "

These clarifications by the SEC and MAS don’t affect the development strategy of our crowdfunding project, as we initially assumed that the release and sale of crypto tokens in exchange for promises of a share in the company or dividends in many countries falls under the regulation of securities legislation and will have to be registered by the relevant way. The decisions of the SEC and MAS only confirm our vision.

Do you have your own pool of investors?

We have our own investors who follow our site and monitor promising projects. However, there are no guarantees that they may be interested in your project, this can’t be expected.

Are your translation services expensive?

Now it’s free. In the future, we’ll try to adhere to the same rule. But we translate only those campaigns that we like. Later we’ll have an automated system of user content, where the users of the platform will help the authors translate their texts, improve them, make artworks and so on for a fee in KickCoins.

What does it mean to "support KickCoins" or "support the Kickonomy"?

This means taking them as payment, giving by them unique content, opportunities, offers, or substantial discounts. By taking KickCoins, you’ll get a lot of support from the users of the platform, you’ll be able to attract investors for free, and much more.

How do authors and platform interact with each other? Who and how controls money?

Money is controlled by a smart contract, in which the author's wallet is initially contributed, to which the collected funds are sent after the end of the campaign. KICKICO can only:

 

  1. pre-end the campaign (successfully) and cancel the campaign.
  2. money is returned to all investors in their wallets, automatically.

 

What do you need to get started: some kind of agreements, information, documents or something else?

To run the project on the platform, you will need copies of the passports of the founders and the general director of the company, bank statements confirming the fact of their existence. If you are a private person, then your scan and bank statement.
